  1. A student is developing a model that shows the steps of protein synthesis. The model should include activities in which three structures?

    1. ribosomes, rough endoplasmic reticulum, Golgi apparatus

    2. nucleus, Golgi apparatus, mitochondria

    3. mitochondria, rough endoplasmic reticulum, nucleus

    4. Golgi apparatus, nucleus, lysosome

    5. nucleus, cytoskeleton, cell membrane

  2. This model of a plant cell shows the structures that are likely to be observed in a typical plant cell. Cells from actual plants may differ from the model if the cells are specialized for specific functions.

    How could this model be changed to best represent a specialized leaf cell?

    1. Remove the cell wall because only cells in plant stems have cell walls.

    2. Remove the mitochondria because leaf cells don't need much energy.

    3. Add tiny hairs because leaf cells need to take in water.

    4. Add chloroplasts because leaves are where photosynthesis occurs.

    5. Remove the plasma membrane because a leaf cell has a cell wall instead of a plasma membrane.

  3. Which of the following processes is directly controlled by the cell nucleus?

    1. Osmosis because the nucleus has nuclear pores that allow water to move in and out.

    2. Energy production because the nucleus contains mitochondria.

    3. Cell membrane assembly because the cell membrane is made from the nuclear envelope.

    4. Waste removal because a steady stream of molecules moves out of the nucleus.

    5. Protein synthesis because DNA in the nucleus contains the instructions for making proteins.

  4. In an investigation, plant cells are placed in water that has been tinted blue. The investigators observe the color of the cells changing to blue. What additional observation would be evidence that the cells maintain homeostasis in response to this change?

    1. The cells burst apart.

    2. The cell membranes shrinks away from the cell walls.

    3. The central vacuole in the cells increases in size.

    4. The cells pumps out water by endocytosis.

    5. The cells pump in additional water until they burst apart.
